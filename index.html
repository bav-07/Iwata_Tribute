Satoru Iwata

"On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."

Satoru Iwata, the 4th President and CEO of Nintendo, established himself as one of the greatest leaders in the history of the video game industry. Throughout his glittering career, he worked on many beloved series, with instrumental contributions to Kirby, Smash Bros. and Pokémon, as well as overseeing the launch of the Nintendo DS and Wii, two of the most successful consoles of all time.

Iwata challenged the notion that video games were not for everyone. His philosophy, that "video games are meant to be just one thing: fun. Fun for everyone," was the driving force that brought forth a new era in gaming.

Born in 1959, in the Hokkaido prefecture of Japan, Iwata studied at the Tokyo Institute of Technology, where he majored in computer science.

Displaying a keen interest in computers and programming, Iwata was coding and creating games from a young age. In 1980, Iwata was invited to join HAL Laboratory, where he would begin working part-time whilst still attending university. HAL Laboratory's first interaction with Nintendo was via HAL completing the development of NES Pinball. This was the beginning of HAL's close relationship with Nintendo.

Encouraged by Nintendo's President of the time Yamauchi, Iwata became the President of HAL Laboratory in 1993, at a time when HAL was struggling with a debt of ¥1.5 billion. In only 6 years, Iwata successfully restored HAL's finances and brought success. During this time, he made instrumental contributions to multiple beloved series, working on Kirby, Earthbound and Pokémon.

In 2002, Satoru Iwata became the first president of Nintendo not to hail from the Yamauchi family, whom held the reins of the esteemed Japanese gaming company since its inception in 1889.
